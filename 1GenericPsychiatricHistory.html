Station : General psychiatric history and Psychiatric review of systems


The instructions for this station are likely to ask you to focus on one part of the history, e.g. presenting complaint and history of presenting complaint, social history. personal history.


In taking a psychiatric history, it Is especially Important to put the patient at ease and to be seen to be sensitive, tactful, and empathic.


Before starting


  • Introduce yourself to the patient.
  • Ensure that he is comfortable. Make some general comments to put him at ease and build it rapport.


Identifiers


  • Name, age, and mode of referral (if not already provided).


Presenting complaint and history of presenting complaint


  • Ask mainly open questions, e.g. Can you tell me why you've come to the hospital today? Try to form a diagnostic hypothesis and to validate or falsify it by asking further questions (IogicoÂ·deductive approach). Based on your hypothesis use the questions from appropriate history station [e.g. You think that this patient might be having depression, ask questions from the depression history station]
  • Ask about:
    • The onset and duration of symptoms.
    • The effect the symptoms are having on the patient's everyday life
    • Any treatments so far.
  • If you have not done so already, ask screening questions [Psychiatric Review of Systems] â if the screen turns positive then use questions from appropriate history station.


Depression

    • Have you felt sad or depressed?
    • Have you lost interest in previously pleasurable activities

                               

Mania / Hypomania

    • In the past week, have you felt very cheerful or unusually irritable or over-talkative
    • Have you felt unusually high level of energy and decreased need for sleep


Anxiety

    • GAD - Do you worry excessively? Often feel your worry is out of control?
    • Panic attack - Do you have anxiety attacks?
    • Agoraphobia - Do you avoid going certain places because you are fearful of having a panic attack?
    • Social anxiety - Are you uncomfortable in social situations?
    • Phobias â Do you have any special fears such as fear of insects or flying?
    • OCD - Do you have any unusual or repetitive thoughts that you know are silly but you cannot stop thinking about? Do you feel there are certain rituals you have to do?
    • PTSD - Do you painful memories or dreams of a terrible experience, such as a an accident?


Psychosis

    • Have you had a waking dream like experience?
    • Have you had strange experiences?
    • Do you hear of see things that other people can't hear or see?
    • Do you ever feel that people are bothering you or trying to harm you?
    • Do you feel that strangers look at you or make comments about you?

               

Substance Use

    • Do you use alcohol, tobacco or any other recreational drug?


Somatic symptom disorder â Do you worry a lot about your health?


Cognitive disorders - Do you have difficulty with your memory or orientation?


Risk questions

    • Suicide - Have you ever thought that life is not worth living? Ever tried to hurt yourself?
    • Homicidal ideations â Do you carry a lot of anger or frustration inside your self? Have you ever thought of hurting someone?
    • If appropriate â getting lost, personal safety, safety of children in home, financial management        


Informant history


    • If the patient is accompanied by a relative, friend, or carer, an informant history should be taken at this stage. Illness specific questions as in HPI can be asked, apart from informants observations about change in patients behaviour.  


Past psychiatric history


    • Previous episodes of illness.
    • Previous treatments and their outcomes.
    • Previous admissions, formal and informal.
    • History of neglect or selfÂ·harm.
    • History of violence.


Past medical history


    • Current illness:
    • Acute illness.
    • Chronic illness.
    • Vascular risk factors.
    • Past and childhood illnesses, including head injury.
    • Surgery.


Drug history/current treatments


    • Psychological treatments.
    • Prescribed medication.
    • Recent changes in prescribed medication.
    • Over-the-counter drugs.
    • Allergies.


Substance use


    • Alcohol.
    • Tobacco.
    • Recreational drugs.
    • NB: Further questioning to establish dependence may be required if alcohol use and/or recreational drug use is high (see Alcohol history).


Family history


    • Determine if anyone in the family has suffered from psychiatric illness or attempted suicide, e.g. Has anyone in the family ever had a nervous breakdown?
    • Partner: age or age at death, occupation, health.
    • Children: age or age at death, occupation, health.
    • Quality of relationships and atmosphere in the home.
    • Recent events in the family.


Personal history


    • Birth.
    • Developmental milestones.
    • Childhood: emotional problems, serious illnesses, prolonged separation from parents.
    • Educational achievement.
    • Occupational history.
    • Psychosexual history: past and present partners (including same sex partners), quality of relationships, frequency of sexual intercourse, sexual problems, physical or sexual abuse,
    • Forensic history, e.g. Have you ever had problems with the police or with the law?
    • Religious or spiritual orientation, e.g. Do you believe there is something beyond us, like God?


Social history


    • Self-care.
    • Social support.
    • Housing.
    • Finances.
    • Typical day.
    • interests and hobbies.
    • Predominant mood and premorbid personality (sometimes included under personal history).


After taking the History


    • Ask the patient if there is anything he might add that you have forgotten to ask about.
    • Summarise your findings and offer a differential diagnosis.
    • Thank the patient.
