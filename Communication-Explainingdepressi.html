Explaining depression


Scenario: A 55 year old man who is not sleeping well and feels tired. His wife passed away 7 months ago. You have just assessed him and diagnosed him as having major depression. Explain the diagnosis to the patient. 


Checklist:


Uses redirection skills

  • Thanks patient for sharing their story with him / her.
  • Explains that the information has provided fair idea about what is going on.


Gets patients permission to discuss the diagnosis - est, rapport

  • "Would it be okay if we discuss what is my assessment of what might be going on?


Explains depression

  • Explains depression and differentiates from normal sadness
    • 'It seems that you may be having depression.'
    • 'Sadness  is  a  natural  response  to  stressful  events  in  life.'
    • 'Whereas, depression  is  an  illness  in  which there is persistent sadness that one cannot snap out of.'
    • 'It is a common disorder and affects 1 in 5 people.'
  • Explain symptoms 
    • 'Patients with depression may feels sad, tearful or irritable.'
    • 'They lose interest in activities that were once pleasurable.'
    • 'The can lose sleep, appetite and lose weight.'
    • 'This can make them feel exhausted and fatigued.'
    • 'They can have difficulty concentrating and making decisions.'
    • 'And may have feelings of excessive guilt and negative emotions, which they canâ€™t shake off.'
    • 'If not treated in time they can also develop suicidal ideations.'
  • If asked about the cause of depression
    • 'There is no one cause of depression. It can be caused by multiple different factors.'
    • 'There are not specific genes identified for depression, genetic vulnerability can be inherited. It can run (not always) in families. Interaction of this genetic vulnerability with stressful life events may precipitate an episode of depression.' 
    • 'Financial  problems,  death  of  a  loved  one,  relationship  break  down, social isolation are examples of stressful like events that can contribute to depression.' 
    • 'Current understanding is that depression is a brain based disorder in which changes in the levels of serotonin occur in the areas of the brain that control mood and thinking."
    • 'In certain cases the depression may be brought on by systemic problems such as heart problem, stroke or thyroid disorder.'
  • if asked about how it is diagnosed 
    • 'There is no test to diagnose depression. It is diagnosed based on the history and evaluation of the mental state. However, doctors may advised test to rule out systemic problems as the cause of depression.'
  • If asked if it treatable 
    • 'Outlook is very promising.  Most people benefit from treatment.' 
  • If asked what is the treatment 
    • 'The pharmacological treatment involves the use of anti-depressant medications, which work by modulating levels of serotonin and other neurotransmitters,'
    • 'Tablets treatment combined with talk therapy is found to be more effective than either of them alone.'
    • 'Getting more information about depression can help you manage yourself better. Good sleeping habits and regular exercise are supportive to the antidepressant treatment, much the same way as exercise if helpful in reducing blood pressure in addition to antihypertensive medications.'


Assesses patients understanding?

  • 'Does this make sense to you?' 


Summary 

  • Thank patient 
  • Questions? Concerns?
  • Suggest further tests


Examiners global rating [5 marks] based on

  • Good question style [open / closed / clarifying]
  • Establishment of rapport
  • Use of empathic statements
  • Clarity [non-jargon, pace and tone]
  • Courtesy
  • Non-judgmental attitude
  • Appropriate body language
  • Overall competence
