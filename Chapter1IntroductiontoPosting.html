In order to make this a beneficial learning experience, this syllabus has been prepared outlining the learning objectives. The Psychiatry Clerkship at PSMC, Karamsad will be 2 weeks rotation in the 2nd year and 2 weeks rotation in the 3rd year. The objective of the clerkship is to provide a broad view of clinical psychiatry enabling student to participate in a variety of patient care settings.  Because of logistical considerations, the clinical experience of students varies depending on clinical placement. We encourage all of you to participate actively in the clerkship.  The faculty and staff of the department are interested in you and welcome your feedback.  If at any time during the rotation you have a problem or concern, please don’t hesitate to call my office.


NOTE:  Schedules etc. would be communicated through notice board or announcements and not through this resource.


Rules and Expected behaviours


  • Postings will be from 8:30AM to 11:30AM.
  • History taking
    • Prior permission must be taken from the clinician before engaging with any patient - the clinician would brief you about the case before proceeding.
    • Setting of interview - interview must be done while ensuring sufficient privacy e.g. inside the interview rooms with one or two students and in presence of patients relatives with the patients permission.
    • Respect of the patient, relatives and their values and belief systems must be maintained.
    • Confidentiality of patient.
  • Students are expected to present cases in group of 2 students, roll number wise.
  • Minimum 75% attendance during posting is mandatory.
  • Those having less than 50% attendance would not be allowed to appear for ward ending exam.
  • Maintain professional conduct
    • Be compassionate and respectful: Under no circumstances is it acceptable to make fun of the patients.
    • Do not worry if a patient asks you to leave the room: Some patients might feel ashamed about their condition or specific details and do not want students involved in their care.
    • Patients may lie to you
      • Try to remain objective.        
      • Report back what the patient told you and explain to your team why you think the patient may have lied.
    • Do not confront the patient
    • Under no circumstances confront patients about their delusions: It is more advisable to express confusion and ask for clarification in the politest way possible (e.g., “I am confused, I thought ….; could you help me clarify that?”).
    • Try not to be easily offended: Psychiatric patients may speak to you in a disrespectful manner. Remind yourself that it has nothing to do with you, and is most likely because the patient is in a bad place at the moment.
    • Try to de-escalate tense situations: Remain calm and try your best to calm down agitated patients.
    • Be aware of your language and affect: If you look anxious, patients might feel similar and respond to that with aggression.
    • Do not hesitate to ask for help if you are unsure about something.
    • Never forget to do a risk assessment: While it may be uncomfortable, it is absolutely essential to ask patients about thoughts of suicide, self-harm, and thoughts of harming others.
    • Remember that safety comes first: Be cautious with patients who have a history of violent/aggressive behavior. Ask the staff for guidance when you are about to interview a patient with such a history.
    • Humour, when appropriately applied in the clinical setting, can help build patient rapport and trust. Remember, though, that this approach might not be applicable to every psychiatric patient and therefore should be used with caution.


How teaching will be conducted?


  • Didactic teaching would be conducted in the ward side teaching room - next to chest medicine ward on second floor
  • After didactic teaching is over students are supposed to observe and evaluate patients in the OPD no 110 on ground floor
  • Cases need to be presented to consultants or SR and get marked on the same day


How will your performance be assessed?


  • Total assessment for psychiatry posting is out of 30.
  • Marks out of 5 would be awarded for case presentations
  • 10 marks OSCE would be conducted on the last but one day of the posting.
  • 15 marks viva would be conducted on the last day of the posting.
  • Minimum 50% needs to be achieved to pass in the term ending exam.
