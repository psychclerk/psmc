Evaluation of a patient with elevated mood

[Expanded with example questions to ask and MSE findings to expect]


Scenario: A 25 year old woman has been brought into the clinic by her mother because she has been behaving strangely since 1 week and has been talking too much.


Checklist:


Introduction

  • Introduces self
  • Explains purpose of history and gains consent


Focused history of presenting complaint

  • Open question “What brings you here today? What sorts of problems have you been having”
  • Complaint: onset, sudden/gradual? preceding event?


Mood history: Mania

  • Mood
    • Asks about mood?
      • ‘Of late how has your mood been?’                        
    • Asks about how long they have been feeling this way?
      • ‘How long has it been that way?’                        
    • Asks clarifying questions for elevated or or irritable mood?
      • ‘Are you staying more happy than usual?’
      • ‘Do you get irritated more often than usual?’                        
  • Energy        
    • Asks the patient about change in energy level?
      • ‘How have your energy levels been?’
      • ‘How long has that been?’                        
  • Interest
    • Asks how the patient has been using their increased energy levels? [increased goal directed activity]
      • ‘How have you been spending your new-found energy?’
      • ‘Like…. have you been…’
      • Work - ‘Working more?’, ‘Taking up new projects or responsibilities?’
      • Social - ‘Talking to a lot of people?’
      • Psychomotor - ‘Feeling restless or agitated?'                        
  • Sleep
    • Asks the patient about their sleep?
      • 'How is your sleep?'                        
    • Asks clarifying questions about decreased need for sleep?
      • ‘How many hours of sleep have you been getting?’
      • ‘Do you feel rested with that many hours of sleep?’                        
  • Concentration        
    • Asks the patient about change in ability to concentrate?
      • ‘How has your ability to focus been recently?’
      • ‘… can you focus long enough so that you could finish your tasks… like listening to a song or watching s television episode?’                        
  • Pace of thoughts
    • Asks about increased talkativeness?
      • ‘Have you been more talkative than usual?’                        
    • Asks about racing of thoughts?
      • 'Have you been experiencing that your thoughts are going very fast?’                        
    • Asks about flights of ideas?
      • 'Do you feel that your thoughts are jumping from one to another?’                        
  • Self-worth
    • Asks the patient about how they feel about self?
      • ‘Do you feel you are different than other people?..In what ways?’                        
  • Psychotic symptoms
    • Ask for presence of grandiose ideas?
      • ‘Do you feel you have special power or ability?
      • ‘Do you feel that your life has a special purpose?
      • ‘Do you feel you have a special relationship with a celebrity or god?
    • Asks for other delusions?
      • ‘Do you feel people talking about you?
      • ‘Do you feel people are trying to harm you?’
    • Any other if required                        
    • Checks conviction of delusions?
      • ‘Have you told about these to others?’
      • ‘And what has been their reactions? Do they believe you?’
      • Or ‘Do you feel these thoughts are 100% true?’                        
    • Asks for hallucinations?
      • ‘Have you been having odd experiences like hearing voices that others cannot hear?’
      • ‘Any other odd experiences that cannot be easily explained?’                        
  • Risks        
    • For Elevated mood, risk assessment can be done at this juncture, but to maintain format it has been shown below                        
  • Effect on functioning
    • Asks for the impact of illness on day to day functioning?
      • 'What has been the impact of all this on your day to day functioning?'        


Focused Psych ROS

  • Psychosis – already done above
  • Depression – Have you had any episode lasting at least 2 weeks where you felt depressed or loss of interest?
  • Anxiety - 'Do you worry or lot or have panic attacks?'
  • Any other if required


Risks – SSS S/H

  • Screening - ‘Since the time that you have been feeling [MOOD], have you done something that could cause trouble for you or your family?’ … Like ….
  • Spending – ‘Have you been spending a lot?’
  • Substance – ‘Have you used alcohol or recreational drugs?’
  • Sexual – ‘Has there been a change in your sexual desire?’
  • Suicidal – ‘Have you had thoughts of hurting yourself?’
  •  Homicidal – ‘Do you have a lot of anger or frustration? Have you thought of hurting someone?


Past psychiatric history – esp depression, mania, suicide attempt


  • 'Have you ever had any psychiatric illness / consultation in the past?' [Explore in detail if yes]


Family history – esp. Depression, suicide, psychosis


  • 'Has any of your blood relative suffered from any psychiatric illness or attempted suicide?'


Past medical history – including allergies, if required developmental history

  • 'Have your doctor diagnosed you with any medical condition?'
  • 'Do you have any allergies?'


Drug and Substance use

  • Prescription drugs
    • 'Are you on any prescription medications?'
  • OTC and CAM drugs
    • 'Are you using any OTC or Alternative medications?'
  • Substance of abuse – alcohol, tobacco, opioids, cannabis etc
    • 'Do you use any substance like alcohol, tobacco or recreational drugs?'


Psychosocial History

  •  Education
    • 'How far did you go in school / college?'
    • 'How did you do in school / college?'
    • 'Did you have any trouble with peers / teachers?'
  • Occupation
    • 'What did you do after finishing school / college?'
    • 'What jobs have you held and for how long?'
    • 'Can you tell me about your current job?'
  • Family and friends, social support [who is providing], financial support, housing situation
    • 'Can you tell me about your family and friends?'
    • 'Can you tell me about who is providing emotional support to you at this moment?'
    • 'Do you have trouble making ends meet?'
  • Legal history?
    • 'Have you had any convictions or legal troubles?'


Insight

  • 'Do you think you need help?'
  • ‘Would you agree if I say your current condition may be explained by an illness?’
  • 'Could it be a psychiatric illness?'
  • 'Would you be willing to accept treatment?'


Summary

  • Thank patient
  • Questions? Concerns?
  • Suggest further tests


MSE Findings in a patient with elevated mood


Appearance and behaviour: The patient appears excited, restless, hyperactive, and dressed in colourful or dramatic clothing. They may be engaging and entertaining but may also be hostile and uncooperative.


Speech: Rapid rate, increased volume, increased quantity, and difficult to interrupt.


Mood: “Great.”


Affect: Expansive, euphoric, labile at times with rapid shifts to irritability; sometimes alternating with intense dysphoria.


Thought process: Pressured, with flight of ideas.


Thought content: Grandiose delusions of great wealth and intelligence, feelings of having special powers, such as clairvoyance, or ideas of reference.


Suicidality: May be present, especially in mixed manic states with depressive symptoms.


Homicidality: Typically denies.


Perceptual: Auditory, visual, or command hallucinations may occur with psychotic features.


Cognition: Alert and oriented, with variable immediate and delayed recall, depending on the patient’s ability to focus or cooperate. The patient may be easily distracted, with poor attention and concentration. Thinking is not concrete or abstract but may be bizarre and incoherent at times.


Insight: Impaired. Patients may like the symptoms of mania and do not recognize the need for treatment.


Judgment: Impaired. Manic patients often do not understand how their symptoms affect behaviour or other people. The patient may be hypersexual and may repeatedly attempt to touch the examiner.


Reliability: Limited. Patients experiencing manic episodes may not be able to give accurate information about past medical, psychiatric, personal or substance-abuse histories.
