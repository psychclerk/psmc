Evaluation of a patient with Anxiety

[Expanded with example questions to ask and MSE findings to expect]


Scenario:


Checklist:


Introduction

  • Introduces self
  • Explains purpose of history and gains consent


Focused history of presenting complaint

  • Open question “What brings you here today? What sorts of problems have you been having”
  • Complaint: onset, sudden/gradual?, preceding event?


Anxiety history


Step 1 Screening questions - if no hypothesis is generated based on the evaluation of presenting complaints, use questions


  • GAD - Do you worry excessively?
  • Panic attacks - Do you have anxiety attacks?
  • Agoraphobia - Do you avoid going certain places because you are fearful of having a panic attack?
  • Social phobia - Are you uncomfortable in social situations?
  • Specific Phobias – Do you have any specific fears such as fear of insects or flying?
  • OCD - Do you have any unusual or repetitive thoughts that you know are silly but you cannot stop thinking about? Do you feel there are certain rituals you have to do?
  • PTSD - Do you painful memories or dreams of a terrible experience, such as a an accident?


Step 2 Specific questioning based on hypothesis


If GAD screen positive - Use AND IC REST mnemonic to evaluate for the DSM criteria for GAD


  • Assess for core symptom of GAD
    • Anxious or worried on most days about day to day events or activities - Do you worry most of the time? What kinds of things do you worry about?
    • No control over worry - Do you find that the worry is hard to control?
    • Duration > 6 months - Since how long have you been feeling like this?
  • Assess for associated sx of GAD
    • Irritability - Do you find that you are more irritable than usual?
    • Concentration difficulty - Do you have any trouble with your concentration?
    • Restlessness - Do you feel restless or can sit still?
    • Energy decreased - Do you feel more tired than usual?
    • Sleep disturbance -How is your sleep?
    • Tension in muscles - Do your muscles feel tense?


If Panic attack screen positive


  • Assess patients understanding of  panic attacks
    • A panic attack is a sudden rush of fear and nervousness in which your heart pounds, you get short of breath, and you’re afraid you’re going to lose control or even die. Has that ever happened to you? Can you describe in your words what you experience during attacks?
  • Assess symptoms of panic attack? 4 out of 13 symptoms are required
    • During the attack do you..
      • ..feel palpitation, chest pain, difficulty in breathing, or choking sensation?  
      • ..feel any sweating, tremulousness, dizziness, tingling sensations or hot/cold sensation?
      • ..feel like you are going to go crazy or going to die?
      • ..feel as if you are in a dream or you are able to watch yourself from outside?
  • Assess for out of the blue nature of panic attacks in panic disorder
    •  When you have these attacks do they come out of the blue or are they triggered by something?
    • Triggered panic attacks may signify social phobia, if the trigger is a social situation; PTSD, if the trigger is a flashback; agoraphobia, if the trigger is a hard-to-escape place; or a specific phobia with a variety of possible triggers. Organic conditions may also be ppt by certain triggers, such as angina brought on by exercise.
  • Assess for other qualities of panic attack
    • How long does the attack lasts?
    • How does it get better?
  • Assess for panic disorder
    • Repeated attacks - Since how long have you been having such attacks? How frequently? Have they been worsening?
    • Associated worry - Are you persistently concerned about having another attack? What do you fear might happen?
    • Avoidance behaviours - Have you been avoiding certain situations due to fear of attacks? Explore for agoraphobia if positive.


If Agoraphobia screen positive


  • Do you have problems with crowded places? Buses or Trains? Restaurants? Bridges?
  • Do you get anxious when you leave home?


If Social phobia screen positive


  • Assess patients understanding of social phobia
    • I mean situations such as public speaking, asking questions in front of a class, or being at a party or in a meeting.  
  • Assess for social phobia symptoms
    • Duration - When did it start?
    • Situations - Can you give examples of situations which you find difficult?
    • Anxiety
      • What thoughts do you have during in these situations? (we are looking for fear of social performance)
      • How uncomfortable do you get? Have you ever had panic attacks in these situations?
    • Avoidance - Do you try to avoid these situations?


If Specific phobia screen positive


  • Explore the fear of specific object or situation - Can you tell me more about it?
  • Asses for fear or anxiety by exposure - When exposed to these situation do you experience an immediate sense of fear or anxiety?
  • Assess for avoidance - Do you find yourself taking steps to avoid this? What are they?


If OCD screen positive


  • Clarifies nature of obsessions
    • Can you tell me more about these thoughts?
    • Do you find them intrusive?
    • Where do they come from? Are they your own thoughts? ( c.f. Thought insertion in schizophrenia where the thoughts come from an outside source)
    • Do you think these thoughts are senseless and should not occur?
    • Resistance: Do you try to stop them?
    • Do they may you feel anxious?
  • Asks for associated compulsive behaviours  
    • What do you do to relieve the anxiety?
    • Does it make you feel less anxious?
    • What would happen if you didn’t perform the action?
    • Do you have any other ways of neutralising the distressing thoughts?
    • Some people may pray or repeat words or count. Do you do anything like that?
    • How much time in a day is spent on these rituals?
  • Assess for distress
    • How do this affect you?
    • How has this affected your work / relationships?


If PTSD screen positive - use RANA mnemonic to evaluate for the DSM criteria for PTSD


  • Assess nature of traumatic event
    • Can you tell me more about the event? (we a looking for history of a traumatic event that involved death or harm to self or others)
  • Assess for Re-experiencing
    • Do you experience intrusive memories of the experience when you did not want to think about it?
    • Do you have nightmares about the event?
    • Do you ever get flashbacks, like it is happening to you again?
  • Assess for Avoidance
    • Do you find yourself avoiding things that you associate with the memory? May ask about specific activities or situations related to the actual trauma
  • Assess for Negativity (Similar to depression)
    • Since the event
      • ..how does your mood stay most of the time? Any Irritability or recklessness?
      • ..have you been experiencing little interest or pleasure in activities that you used to do earlier?
      • ..how do you feel about yourself?
      • ..and about the world?
      • ..and about the future?
  • Assess for arousal
    • Since the trauma, have you felt hyper and on edge much of the time?
    • Do you startle easily?


In all cases continue with the following


Risks - risk assessment can be done at this juncture, but to maintain format it has been shown below


Effect on functioning

    • 'What has been the impact of all this on your day to day functioning?'


Collateral history from a reliable informant.


Focused Psych ROS


  • Depression – Have you had any episode lasting at least 2 weeks where you felt depressed or loss of interest?
  • Manic symptoms - 'In the past week, have you felt very cheerful or unusually irritable or over-talkative?
  • Psychosis – Hallucinations & Delusions? - 'When people are under stress, they complain of hearing voices or believing that other people are doing something to harm him. Do you have such experiences?’
  • Screen for other anxiety disorders


Risk Assessment  


  • Shows empathy before asking about suicidal ideations
    • Use empathy statement like 'You have been going through a lot.'
  • Asked patient about death wishes?
    • 'Have you ever felt like life has become a burden?
    • 'Do you ever feel you go to sleep and never wake up?'
  • Asked patient about suicidal ideations?
    • 'Have you ever had thoughts of harming yourself?' [explore further if yes]
  • Ask for Homicidal ideations – ‘Do you have a lot of anger or frustration? Have you thought of hurting someone?’


Past psychiatric history – esp depression, mania, suicide attempt

  • 'Have you ever had any psychiatric illness / consultation in the past?' [Explore in detail if yes]


Family history – esp. Depression, suicide, psychosis

  • 'Has any of your blood relative suffered from any psychiatric illness or attempted suicide?'


Past medical history – including allergies, if required developmental history

  • 'Have your doctor diagnosed you with any medical condition?'
  • 'Do you have any allergies?'


Drug and Substance use

  • Prescription drugs
    • 'Are you on any prescription medications?'
  • OTC and CAM drugs
    • 'Are you using any OTC or Alternative medications?'
    • 'How much tea and coffee do you drink?'
  • Substance of abuse – alcohol, tobacco, opioids, cannabis etc
    • 'Do you use any substance like alcohol, tobacco or recreational drugs?'


Psychosocial History

  • Education
    • 'How far did you go in school / college?'
    • 'How did you do in school / college?'
    • 'Did you have any trouble with peers / teachers?'
  • Occupation
    • 'What did you do after finishing school / college?'
    • 'What jobs have you held and for how long?'
    • 'Can you tell me about your current job?'
  • Family and friends, social support [who is providing], financial support, housing situation
    • 'Can you tell me about your family and friends?'
    • 'Can you tell me about who is providing emotional support to you at this moment?'
    • 'Do you have trouble making ends meet?'
  • Legal history?
    • 'Have you had any convictions or legal troubles?'


Insight

  • 'Do you think you need help?'
  • 'Do you think you are suffering from depression?'
  • 'Would you be willing to accept treatment?'


Summary 

  • Thank patient
  • Questions? Concerns?
  • Suggest further tests


MSE findings in a patient with anxiety


Appearance and Behaviour: Restless, fidgeting, psychomotor agitation.


Speech: Rate may be increased, but with normal rhythm and volume.


Mood: “Scared,” “nervous,” “out of control.”


Affect: Anxious, irritable, but congruent with stated mood.


Thought process: Pressured, may be more talkative than usual, ruminative, but linear and goal-directed.


Thought content: Preoccupied about somatic complaints, fearful of recurrent panic, phobia or obsessions; patient is afraid of what bad events may happen in the future.


Suicidality: May be present, especially with co-morbid clinical depression; untreated anxiety is a risk factor for suicide.


Homicidality: Rare.


Perception: No auditory, command auditory, or visual hallucinations.


Cognition: Alert and oriented, poor concentration, intact memory, no apparent language deficits or disturbance in abstract thinking, good fund of knowledge and vocabulary.


Insight: Good, although the patient may place exaggerated emphasis on anxiety symptoms


Judgment: Fair. Understands the nature and consequences of his illness. (may be poor in a subtype of OCD patients)


Brief diagnostic criteria for anxiety disorders


Disorder

Main symptoms

Panic disorder with or without agoraphobia (PDAG)

Sudden, severe repeated attacks of anxiety some of which are "out of the blue." Fear of physical symptoms. May be avoidance of feared situations.

Agoraphobia without panic

Avoidance of feared situations (e.g., enclosed spaces, crowds, being away from home) without a history of panic attacks.

Specific phobia (SP)

Fear and avoidance of specific objects/places (e.g., snakes, dogs, sight of blood, heights, storms, tunnels, flying).

Social anxiety disorder (SAD)

Fear and avoidance of public performance or situations of being judged. May be limited or generalized to almost all social interactions.

Obsessive-compulsive disorder (OCD)

Unwanted, uncontrollable, repetitive thoughts (obsessions) and repetitive neutralizing actions (compulsions).

Post-traumatic stress disorder (PTSD)

Re-experiencing of a severe traumatic  event through dreams, flashbacks and intense emotion with increased arousal and avoidance of reminders of the trauma.

Acute stress disorder (ASD)

Symptoms similar to PTSD occurring immediately after an extremely traumatic event.

Generalized anxiety disorder (GAD)

Persistent, uncontrollable worry lasting at least six months.

Anxiety disorder due to general medical condition

Symptoms of anxiety that are a direct physiological consequence of a general medical condition.

Substance-induced anxiety disorder

Symptoms of anxiety that are a direct physiological consequence of a drug of abuse, a medication or toxin exposure.

Anxiety disorder not otherwise specified

Anxiety symptoms not meeting the criteria for any of the specific disorders above.
