Eliciting obsessions and compulsions



Asking about obsessions 
  • Do you have any recurring thoughts, images or impulses that you cannot get out of your mind (recurrent, intrusive)?
  • Can you tell me more about these thoughts? (Focus is not simple day to day worries)
  • How often do they occur (persistence)?
  • Where do they come from? Are they your own thoughts? (own mind)
  • Do they cause distress? In what way? (cause anxiety)
  • Is there anything you try to do to stop these thoughts or reduce the anxiety? (resistance)

Asking about compulsions
  • Do you ever spend a lot of time doing same thing again and again even though you have already done it?
  • Can you tell me more about these?
  • Do you spend a lot of time washing repeatedly, even though you know that you are clean?
  • Do you have to keep checking things that you know you have already done?
  • Do you have to touch or count things many times?
  • Do you have any other rituals or superstitions?
  • What happens when you try to stop doing these?

Duration
  • How long have you been feeling this way?
  • How frequently do you get these thoughts? How frequently do you have to do these behaviours?
  • How much time of your day is occupied by these thoughts or behaviours? 
  • How often do you try to divert your attention away from these thoughts as they enter your mind? How much of an effort do you make to resist the compulsions?

Insight 
  • What do you think might have caused this? 
  • Do you think your concerns or behaviours are reasonable?
  • What do you think would happen if you did not perform the behaviours? Are you convinced something would really happen?

Distress
  • How is it affecting your life?
  • How do you manage to cope?

Diagnosis and Differentials

  • DURATION = NONE SPECIFIED
  • DISTRESS = 
    • Time-consuming [> 1-hr per day]
    • Subjective distress 
    • Impairment in social, occupation or other areas of functioning 
  • DIFFERENTIALS
    • Organic         
      • Substance (temporal correlation)
        • Amphetamine and other stimulants 
        • Cocaine 
      • Medications (temporal correlation)
        • Medical stimulants for ADHD
        • Levodopa used for PD
        • Clozapine 
        • Olanzapine 
        • Risperidone 
        • Quetiapine 
      • Medical 
        • Look for neurological signs of Basal ganglia pathology
          • Hypokinetic 
            • Bradykinesia + Tremor / Rigidity / Postural instability
          • Hyperkinetic
            • Tremors / Chorea / Athetosis/ Ballism / Dystonia / Vocal or Motor Tics
        • Look for neurological signs of frontal pathology (Perseveration may appear as repetitive behavior)
          • Luria 3 step 
          • Go-no-go
          • Primitive reflexes
        • Pointer 
          • OCD begins before 30
          • Anyone with onset after 30 – red flag for organic pathology
        • Listing 
          • Tourette’s 
          • Sydenham’s Chorea
          • Huntington’s Chorea
          • Neuroacanthocytosis
          • Parkinson’s disease 
          • Dystonia 
          • Post head injury
          • Post encephalitic 
          • Post HIE 
          • Fahr’s syndrome
          • Infarction of globus pallidus 
          • TLE 
          • SLE 
          • VCFS
          • NPH
          • Frontal lobe syndrome 
    • Psychiatric (other disorders with repetitive thoughts or behaviors)
      • simple day to day worries – GAD 
      • preoccupation with defect in appearance – Body dysmorphic 
      • preoccupation with having an illness – illness anxiety disorder 
      • difficulty discarding possessions – Hoarding 
      • hair-pulling – Trichotillomania 
      • excoriation and other respective skins manipulations– skin-pricking disorder, Body focused repetitive behavior disorders
      • ritualized eating – eating disorder
      • preoccupation with substance or gambling – substance or addictive disorders 
      • recurrent impulses (set fire or steal) – as in disruptive, impulse control or conduct disorders 
      • guilty ruminations aka obsessional brooding – Depression, differentiated by presence of other depressive symptoms, but depression is often co-morbid with depression!
      • sexual urges or fantasies – paraphilias. Pt with sexual obsessions would find them ego-dystonic and would not act upon them [like masturbation], they would also try to avoid the object of the urge [e.g. avoid children if having obsessions of sexually assaulting children] and would not try to hide their sexual history. 
      • repetitive stereotypes – either ASD or stereotypic movement disorder or ID or dementia 
      • Psychotic symptoms leading to repetitive behaviours – differentiated by presence of other psychotic symptoms which will be absent in OCD. In OCD with poor or absent insight other psychotic symptoms will be absent. 
      • ‘obsessed’ with detail and feel ‘compelled’ to do things perfectly – OCPD, differentiated by the ego-syntonic nature of the thoughts and behaviours [typically see nothing wrong with them, and in fact may even value them]
  • TIC SPECIFIERS
    • current or prior tic disorder
  • INSIGHT SPECIFIER 
    • How is insight assessed in OCD? (ref Y-BOCS)
      • Based on the patients’ beliefs surrounding the concerns and actions [that they are senseless or excessive] 
    • Good insight 
      • Readily acknowledges absurdity or excessiveness of thoughts or behaviours
    • Poor insight 
      • Maintains that thoughts or behaviours are not unreasonable or excessive, but acknowledges validity of contrary evidence (i.e., overvalued ideas present)
    • Absent insight or delusional beliefs 
      • Definitely convinced that concerns and behaviour are reasonable, unresponsive to contrary evidence.


Presentation

Patients with OCD often take their complaints to other physicians rather than psychiatrists. 


Symptoms pattern

Presentation of obsessions and compulsions is heterogeneous in adults and in children and adolescents. OCD has four major symptom patterns.

Obsession

Emotional response

Compulsion

Contamination

Fear of contamination (things which are difficult to avoid e.g., faeces, urine, dust, germs)

Anxiety (common)

Shame

Disgust

Washing (hands or body) or compulsive avoidance (not leaving home for fear of germs)

Doubt 

Doubt (forgetting to turn off stove or lock properly)

Anxiety 

Low confidence

Guilty

Checking (trips back to the home to check)

Intrusive 

Sexual or aggressive thought

Obsessive suicidal ideation 

Feeling of condemnation (strong disapproval) for self 

Usually no obsession, but may report to police or confess to a priest 

Symmetry

Need for symmetry or precision

Anxiety

Slowness (hours to bathe or shave their face)
